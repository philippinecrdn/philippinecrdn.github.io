What is Europe?

Composition

Europe is a continent that is separated from the Americas by the Atlantic ocean.

It is composed of 50 countries.

History

Europe has been a major component of global trade before the United States were created.

It has also been the land of major empires such as the Roman empire, to name one of the oldest, but also the French empite, the British Empire and the third Reich.

These empires were built because of colonisations.

Thus, it led to many wars and other conflicts between European countries but also between them and other countries from other continents.

The Third Reich is the one that caused World War II.

It was ruled by Adolph Hitler and was at the origin of the genocides of the Holocaust and of the Gyspy Genocide.

The end of World War II created a need for European countries to feel closer to each other and to take advantage of the continent resources.

This is among other motivations, what led to the creation of the European Union.

The European Union (EU)

Although the EU is not a federation in the strict sense, it is far more than a free-trade association such as ASEAN or Mercosur, and it has certain attributes associated with independent nations: its own flag, currency (for some members), and law-making abilities, as well as diplomatic representation and a common foreign and security policy in its dealings with external partners.

In 1950, the French Foreign Minister Robert SCHUMAN proposed pooling the production of coal and steel in Western Europe and setting up an organization for that purpose that would bring France and the Federal Republic of Germany together and would be open to other countries as well.

The following year, the European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC) was set up when six members - Belgium, France, West Germany, Italy, Luxembourg, and the Netherlands - signed the Treaty of Paris.

In 1957, envisioning an "ever closer union," the Treaties of Rome were signed creating the European Economic Community (EEC) and the European Atomic Energy Community (Euratom), and the six member states strove to eliminate trade barriers among themselves by forming a common market.

In 1967, the institutions of all three communities were formally merged into the European Community (EC), creating a single Commission, a single Council of Ministers, and the body known today as the European Parliament.

In 1973, the first enlargement of the EC took place with the addition of Denmark, Ireland, and the UK.

The 1980s saw further membership expansion with Greece joining in 1981 and Spain and Portugal in 1986.

The 1992 Treaty of Maastricht laid the basis for further forms of cooperation in foreign and defense policy, in judicial and internal affairs, and in the creation of an economic and monetary union - including a common currency.

This further integration created the European Union (EU), at the time standing alongside the EC.

In 1995, Austria, Finland, and Sweden joined the EU/EC, raising the total number of member states to 15.

A new currency, the euro, was launched in world money markets on 1 January 1999; it became the unit of exchange for all EU member states except Denmark, Sweden, and the UK.

Ten new countries joined the EU in 2004 - Cyprus, the Czech Republic, Estonia, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Malta, Poland, Slovakia, and Slovenia. Bulgaria and Romania joined in 2007 and Croatia in 2013, but the UK withdrew in 2020.

Current membership stands at 27.

Tips to travel more sustainably

Farther is not better

Your country may have to much to offer that most of the time you don't really need to go across oceans to see something incredible.

The longer the better

Taking a six-hour flight just to stay 3 nights in a city is not really beneficial to the environment.

Consider the train rather than the plane

While taking a train in the USA can feel a little bit like a loss of time, in Europe it is pretty easy to take the train to go to Italy or Spain from France for example.

It is even possible to go from Belgium or France to England by train with the undersea train.

Adopt the local life

It is most of the time more enlightening to stay at a Bed and Breakfast or at a hostel rather than a Hilton resort.

Mainly because you contribute to the local economy and may discover more unique places compared to touristic places recommanded by every big Hotel of the city.
